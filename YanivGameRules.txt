Yaniv is a very popular Israeli card game, especially among backpackers, soldiers, and students. The goal is to have the **lowest total value in your hand** and successfully call **"Yaniv!"** before anyone else can beat or tie your score. 

### Setup

* 2–5 players (more is possible).
* Standard 54-card deck (52 cards + 2 jokers).
* Deal **5 cards** to each player.
* Place the remaining cards face down as a draw pile.
* Turn over the top card to start the discard pile. 

### Card Values

| Card    | Value      |
| ------- | ---------- |
| Joker   | 0          |
| Ace     | 1          |
| 2–10    | Face value |
| J, Q, K | 10         |


### On Your Turn

You may discard:

1. A single card.
2. Two or more cards of the same rank (e.g., 7♣ 7♥ 7♠).
3. A run of **3 or more consecutive cards in the same suit** (e.g., 4♥ 5♥ 6♥). Aces are low only (A-2-3 is allowed; Q-K-A is not). 

After discarding, draw:

* The top card from the draw pile, **or**
* A card from the discard pile. If the previous player discarded multiple cards, you may usually take only the first or last card of that discard set. 

### Calling "Yaniv"

At the **start of your turn**, if your hand total is at or below the agreed threshold (most commonly **7 points**), you may call **"Yaniv!"** instead of taking a turn. Everyone reveals their cards. 

### Successful Yaniv

If your hand total is lower than everyone else's:

* You score **0 points** for the round.
* Everyone else scores the value of the cards remaining in their hand. 
* If you are successful, you start the next round.

### Assaf (The Trap!)

If **any other player has the same total or a lower total** than the person who called Yaniv:

* The caller gets **their hand value + 30 penalty points**.
* The player who "caught" them still scores their hand value but get to start the next round. This is called **"Assaf"**. 

Example:

* You call Yaniv with 6.
* Another player reveals 5 (or even another 6).
* You get **36 points** (6 + 30 penalty).
* They get their hand value. 

### Winning the Game

Players keep cumulative scores across rounds and finish when they agree (i.e. can be never ending)
* Lowest total score wins. 

### Common Israeli House Rules


* Exact **100, 200, 300, etc** points halve your score.
* Some play with a Yaniv threshold of 5 or 11, instead of 7.
* Jokers are allowed as wild cards in runs. 

A lot of the strategy revolves around estimating what other players are holding and deciding whether your 5–7 point hand is safe enough to risk a Yaniv call—or whether someone is waiting to Assaf you. 😄
